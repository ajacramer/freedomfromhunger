FAQs & Resources

General Campaign FAQs

Q: What is Freedom From Hunger?

Freedom From Hunger is an annual county-wide campaign that raises funds and awareness to support the MontCo Anti-Hunger Network (MAHN) and its 70+ food pantries and meal providers serving Montgomery County residents.

Q: Who organizes the campaign?

Freedom From Hunger is presented by the Valley Forge Tourism & Convention Board (VFTCB) in partnership with MAHN. 100% of donations raised through this campaign directly support MAHN’s hunger-relief programs.

Q: Why was the campaign started?

Freedom From Hunger began in 2015 as a community gift inspired by Pope Francis’ visit to Montgomery County. What started as a symbolic act of service has grown into a major annual effort to fight food insecurity locally.

Donation & Impact FAQs

Q: Where does my donation go?

Every dollar directly funds MAHN’s work — including food distribution, pantry support, capacity-building, and emergency resources for families across Montgomery County.

Q: How many people experience food insecurity in Montgomery County?

More than 80,000 residents face food insecurity annually, including families, seniors, and children.

Q: How much food does the campaign help provide?

Historically, the campaign has funded hundreds of thousands of pounds of food each year. On average, every $1 helps MAHN source and distribute up to 12 pounds of food.

Q: Which food pantries and providers are supported?

More than 70 partners benefit from Freedom From Hunger, including community food pantries, emergency meal providers, food banks. View the full list here.

Ways to Get Involved FAQs

Q: Can I volunteer instead of donating?

Yes. Pantries regularly welcome volunteers. Visit montcoantihunger.org for volunteer opportunities across the county.

Q: Are food donations accepted?

Many pantries accept direct food donations, but monetary donations have the greatest impact because MAHN can purchase food at significantly discounted bulk prices.

Q: Do you offer opportunities for local businesses to participate?

Absolutely! Restaurants, retailers, and service providers can run fundraising promotions, host events, join as campaign partners, or sponsor key initiatives. Visit the Get Involved section for details.

Support & Press FAQs

Q: How can I contact the campaign team?

Email us at hunger@valleyforge.org.

Q: Where can I find the press release or media kit?

You can download the latest press release or access our social media kit using the buttons below.

Q: Where can I find resources if I or someone I know is facing food insecurity?

MAHN provides a county-wide pantry locator, information on assistance programs, and emergency food support. View the Montgomery County Food Resource Guide for help.

Social Media Kit 2025 Press Release

2025 Freedom From Hunger Press Release

The Valley Forge Tourism & Convention Board has launched the 11th annual Freedom From Hunger Food Drive to support the MontCo Anti-Hunger Network (MAHN) and help neighbors experiencing food insecurity across Montgomery County. This year’s goal is to raise $15,000, the equivalent of 180,000 pounds of food.

The campaign kicked off with a kitchen takeover at Emmanuel Lutheran Church in Pottstown, where VFTCB staff and Montgomery County Commissioners helped prepare and serve lunch for local guests. Since 2015, the drive has generated the equivalent of more than 500,000 pounds of food, with demand continuing to rise at pantries county-wide.

  • Goal: $15,000 (180,000 lbs of food)
  • Benefiting more than 70 local pantries and meal providers
  • Supporting over 85,000 residents facing food insecurity each year
  • MAHN has seen a 113% increase in food delivered since 2022
